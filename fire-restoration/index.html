The Hidden Damage After a Fire

Fire Damage Goes Far Beyond What You Can See

The flames may be out, but the damage continues. Smoke particles, soot residue, and water from firefighting efforts cause ongoing deterioration if not addressed quickly by professionals.

Smoke & Soot Penetration

Smoke particles penetrate every surface, cavity, and fabric in your home. Acidic soot corrodes metals, discolours paint, and causes permanent staining if not cleaned within days.

Secondary Water Damage

Firefighting efforts saturate your property with water. Without immediate extraction and drying, this leads to mould growth, warped timber, and further structural damage.

Structural Compromise

Heat weakens timber framing, steel beams, and concrete. A professional structural assessment is essential before any restoration work or re-occupation can begin.
