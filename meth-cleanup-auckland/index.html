The Hidden Danger in Auckland Properties

Meth Contamination Is Invisible — But the Risks Are Real

Methamphetamine residue absorbs into every surface of a property. You can't see it, but it affects everyone living there — especially children. Auckland has one of the highest rates of meth-contaminated properties in New Zealand.

Health Risks to Occupants

Meth residue causes headaches, respiratory problems, skin irritation, nausea, and sleep disturbance. Children are most vulnerable due to smaller body weight and hand-to-mouth contact.

Property Value Impact

A contaminated property cannot legally be rented out and its value drops significantly. Banks may refuse to lend against a property with a positive meth test until decontamination is completed.

Legal Obligations

Landlords have a legal duty to provide a safe, habitable property. Under the Residential Tenancies Act, a meth-contaminated rental must be tested and decontaminated before tenants can occupy it.

NZS 8510:2017 is the New Zealand Standard for testing and decontamination of methamphetamine-contaminated properties. It sets the acceptable level of methamphetamine residue at 1.5 micrograms per 100cm² for properties used as dwellings. The standard covers testing procedures, decontamination methods, validation testing, and clearance certification. All meth testing and decontamination by iDry follows this standard.
